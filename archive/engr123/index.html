        2) inductive hypothesis:

        assume your statement is true for some arbitrary case

        • we know how to go from one to the next one

        3) inductive step

        prove, knowing inductive hypothesis, that the statement is true for any value of k

        • test that this is true for any value of k as example

        • Poisson

          Conditions/Assumptions

          Conditions/assumptions on a Poisson distribution are:
          1. events occur singly
            • each events happens on at a time, not at the same time
            • one car can pass one at a time on a lane
          1. events occur at a constant rate
            • on average
            • probability of an event occurring is the same per unit interval
          1. events occur independent
            • a car passing doesn’t effect the probability of another car passing

          assumptions don’t have to be perfectly meet when applying a mathematical model to a real world application, if the assumptions are good enough then its a good enough model
          counter example where conditions would not meet:
          system failure leading to multiple non independent outages
          • not singly since multiple components turn off at the same time
          • not indeptended since the cause is the outage and one component turning off effects the probability of another
