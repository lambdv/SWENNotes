      DES

      • Data encryption standard
      • 1970 adopted by the US forerunner NIST
      • Small key size (56 bits), easy to crack with brute force (<1 day)
      • New standard would be mutable rounds of DES
        • Triple DES (3 rounds of DES = 3x 56 bit key)
        • Triple DES is predicted to be safe until 2030.

      AES

      • Advanced Encryption Standard
      • 2001 US government standard
      • Competition between 15 designs (1997 - 2000)
      • Rijndael won out as the basis of AES over Blowfish-variant.
      • 128, 192 or 256 bit keys.
        • Having different amount of keys lets you change the amount of time it takes to decrypt depending on the value of the data you’re encrypting.
      • Widely used, Windows 2000 onwards.

      Blowfish

      • Early 1990 potential drop in replacement for DES
      • Was unusual as having a public domain status (you can use it for free)
      • Variable key length from 1 - 448 bits
      • No successful attempt to break it as long as setup key right (they can take a while and people skip setting them up properly).
